YOUR INTERNET PRIVACY MATTER!



Online privacy, also known as internet privacy or digital privacy, refers to how much of your personal, financial and browsing information remains private when you’re online. This has become a growing worry, with browsing history and personal data all potentially at risk when online.

Many people doesn't care about their privacy as much as they have to care, this is so excruciating to see. In our website we will disscuss why your privacy matter and how you prevent your data from getting stolen by irresponsible people.




WHY DOES IT MATTER?





In this fast technological advancement era, your personal data really matter. You don't want someone to know how much you make in a year?, what is your address?, what your phone number is?, what is your browsing history?, who's your best friends? and a lot more sensitive information to get leaked into public.

Online privacy is important for numerous reasons. You don’t want to share details of your personal life with strangers and it’s hard to be sure what personal information is gathered and by whom: information collected by one company might be shared with another.

Your personal information is highly desireable to cyber criminals. After these people steals your data, they may be able to accsess your banking details, obtain credit card or loans, destroy your credit rating. After all that your information will be sold in the internet for 5$.





HOW DO YOU PRVENT YOUR DATA FROM GETTING STOLEN?





1. Secure Your Password

Password are the keys to our digital lives. Cybercriminals employ many tactic to try and crack them. To make this task diffucult for them, use strong, complicated password.

  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), if avaliable, is another way of verifying your identity other than just a username and password. 2FA is comprised of something you know (such as a password), something you have (a mobile phone that a code can be sent to), or something you are (such as fingerprint)
  • Never save passwords on your device. Yes, it’s convenient. Yes, it saves time. But if you need to safely store passwords, look into a secure password manager.

2. Be careful what you do with your phone.

While being cautious with what you do with your phone is always recommended, it can get tiresome. With the vast amount of malware, Trojans, and worms finding sneaky ways to get into your device, it is better to stay protected with a security system that does the work for you.







3. Be Aware Of Your Digital Footprint

Whatever you post online is there forever, even if you delete them from where it was originally posted. A digital footprint is somewhat of a digital paper trail. Forum registrations, videos, photos, just about anything you post will all leave traces of information about yourself online.

4. Familiarize yourself with phising.

Phishers generally attempt to impersonate a well-known organization, such as a banking or financial institution, in order to try to obtain your user credentials, or deliver malware to your computer via suspicious links or attachments in email messages. Never click on attachments or links from unknown senders or from suspicious looking emails. Instead, if you think your account is in jeopardy, go directly to the website in question by typing the address into the URL bar in your browser rather than clicking the link in the message.
